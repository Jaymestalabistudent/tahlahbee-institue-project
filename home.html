Statistics reveal a troubling trend: young fathers from disadvantaged backgrounds are at a higher risk of experiencing family separation.

This separation exacerbates mental health challenges, leading to a cycle of instability that affects both the father and the child.

Research shows that these fathers often struggle with inadequate support systems, contributing to elevated stress levels and diminished emotional well-being.

Early attachment experiences shape our internal working models,which are mental blueprints of ourselves and others.

These blueprints influence our expectations and behaviors in relationships, affecting everything from romance to parenting.
Secure attachments boost our self-esteem and trust in others, while insecure attachments can lead to self-doubt and mistrust.
Tackling these attachment issues is key to enhancing mental health and building healthier relationships , especially for single fathers navigating the challenges of parenthood.
