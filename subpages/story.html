Matt : Separated

Matt had always envisioned a life filled with joy and stability for his three children. However, the COVID-19 pandemic brought unforeseen challenges that tested his resilience and determination.

During the pandemic, Matt and his ex-partner decided to separate, and they created a personal separation agreement to manage their responsibilities and time with the children.

The agreement worked well enough during the lock downs, but as restrictions lifted, the informal arrangement began to falter.
Without the structure of the pandemic restrictions, the agreement became increasingly difficult to maintain.
Matt found himself struggling to keep up with the demands of co-parenting and providing for his children.
He had never paid child support, believing that his contributions in other ways were sufficient. However, this assumption was soon challenged in court.Realizing the gravity of the situation, Matt sought help from Citizens Advice and an advocate who guided him through the complexities of family law.
They explained the importance of a court order to formalize the arrangements and ensure the well-being of his children.

The court proceedings were daunting, and Matt felt overwhelmed by the legal jargon and procedures.

In his own words Matt shared his thoughts on the journey:

"I see the light at the end of the tunnel, but it's been incredibly hard without legal aid. Navigating the legal system on my own has been a steep learning curve. I never realized how crucial a court order was until now. It's not just about me; it's about ensuring my kids have the stability and support they need. The advice from Citizens Advice and my advocate has been invaluable, but the process is still ongoing. I'm hopeful, though. I know that once this is all settled, we'll all be in a better place."

Matt's case is still ongoing, but he remains optimistic. He understands now why a formal court order is necessary and is committed to fulfilling his responsibilities as a father. The journey has been tough, but Matt's determination to provide a better future for his children keeps him moving forward.

Dean never thought life would be like this...

Dean : Divorced

Dean's life took an unexpected turn three years ago when his marriage crumbled.
Despite his love for his two children, the separation from their mother was tumultuous. Unable to reach an amicable agreement, Dean found himself cut off from his children.
Isolated and bereft of guidance, he spent three agonizing years without seeing their faces or hearing their laughter.
The silence was deafening, and the weight of his depression grew heavier with each passing day.


One day, while wandering aimlessly through the city, Dean stumbled upon a Fathers4Justice protest. The sight of other fathers fighting for their rights ignited a spark of hope within him.
He immersed himself in research, discovering avenues for visitation rights and mediation with his ex-partner. Though their communication remained strained, Dean finally managed to secure some access to his children. The reunion, though bittersweet, marked the beginning of a new chapter in his life.


Determined to regain a more significant role in his children's lives, Dean embarked on a journey through the court system, seeking joint custody. Simultaneously, he enrolled in a stress management course to cope with the emotional turmoil.
The path was fraught with challenges, but the thought of being a present and supportive father for his children gave him the strength to persevere. Each day, he edged closer to his goal, driven by the love he held for his family.


In Deans words:
"Those three years without my kids felt like an eternity. I was consumed by loneliness and despair, not knowing where to turn or how to fight back. Seeing that Fathers4Justice protest was a turning point.
It was like a lifeline thrown to a drowning man. Researching and taking steps toward mediation and the court process gave me a purpose and a way to channel my energy positively. I still struggle with stress, but the thought of my children motivates me to keep going. I'm determined to be there for them, no matter how hard it gets."

Joe had always been a man of ambition, but....

Joe : Ex-offender

Joe had always been a man of ambition, but one fateful mistake led him down a path he never imagined. Convicted of a serious crime, he found himself sentenced to 15 years in prison. The day he was taken away was the hardest of his life, not just for him, but for his partner and their two young children. The separation was too much for his partner to bear, and she decided to move on, leaving Joe to face his sentence alone.

The first few months in prison were the darkest for Joe. He felt isolated and hopeless, missing his children terribly. It was during this time that he met Mark, a fellow inmate who had been through a similar ordeal. Mark had been in prison for several years and had managed to maintain a relationship with his own children through an outreach scheme that facilitated visits for inmates and their families.

Mark saw the despair in Joe's eyes and decided to help. He introduced Joe to the outreach scheme, explaining how it worked and how it had changed his own life. Joe was skeptical at first, but with nothing to lose, he decided to give it a try.

The process was not easy. There were forms to fill out, interviews to attend, and approvals to wait for. But Joe was determined. He knew that this was his chance to reconnect with his children and be a part of their lives, even from behind bars. After several months of persistence, Joe's application was approved.

The first visit was emotional. Joe's children were hesitant at first, unsure of what to expect. But as the visits became more regular, they began to open up. They shared stories about school, their friends, and their dreams. Joe listened intently, offering advice and support whenever he could. These visits became the highlight of his life in prison, giving him a sense of purpose and hope.

Now, with only seven years left until his release, Joe feels a renewed sense of determination. He knows that he has a long road ahead, but he is committed to making the most of his time in prison. He continues to participate in the outreach scheme, cherishing every moment he gets to spend with his children. He has also enrolled in educational programs and vocational training, preparing himself for the day he will be reunited with his family.

Joe's journey is far from over, but he is no longer the broken man he once was. With the support of his children and the lessons he has learned, he is ready to face the future with hope and resilience.
Miller : Depression

Life is never that simple catch up with Miller

Miller : Depression

Miller had always envisioned a stable and loving environment for his children. However, life took an unexpected turn when his wife had a severe breakdown during the COVID-19 pandemic. Overwhelmed by the pressures and responsibilities, she made the heart-wrenching decision to give up their children to social services. This left Miller in a state of shock and despair, but he knew he had to fight to bring his children back home. Determined to reunite his family, Miller embarked on a challenging journey to regain custody of his children. He sought help from various organizations and advocates who guided him through the complex legal process. They provided invaluable advice on how to navigate the system and apply for all the support and benefits he was entitled to as a single father. Today, Miller is the sole custodian of his children, providing them with the stable and loving home they deserve. His journey is a testament to the power of determination and the importance of seeking help when faced with life's toughest challenges.
